Camera Scanner · by OWESI STUDIO

Camera Scanner helps you check a room for hidden cameras using four different methods. For the best results, use more than one method and scan slowly and thoroughly.

Getting started

  1. Open the App and grant the permissions it asks for (camera, nearby devices, and on Android 12 and below, location). These are needed for scanning — nothing is uploaded.
  2. Tap Start Scan for a quick wireless sweep, or pick a specific detector.
  3. Move slowly around the room, pointing your phone at suspicious objects (smoke detectors, sockets, mirrors, vents, clocks, chargers).

The four detectors

1. Wireless Scan

Looks for nearby Wi-Fi and Bluetooth devices that may be wireless cameras. Useful when a hidden camera is connected to a network. A result is a possible match, not a certainty — check the device name and location.

2. Magnetic Detector

Many cameras contain magnets and electronics that disturb the magnetic field. Move your phone slowly near an object; a sharp rise above the surrounding level may indicate hidden electronics. Large appliances and metal can also raise the reading, so use this as a hint, not proof.

3. Infrared Detector

Some night-vision cameras emit infrared light you can't see with your eyes. Turn off the room lights, start the detector, and look through your phone's camera for small bright dots or glows.

4. Lens Detector

Camera lenses reflect light. Hold your phone close to a suspected spot; the detector highlights bright reflective points that could be a lens. Slowly scan surfaces from different angles.

Tips for a thorough check

  • Scan in a dark room for the infrared and lens methods.
  • Pay special attention to items facing a bed, sofa, or bathroom.
  • Unplug or cover anything you cannot verify, and trust your instincts.
  • For high-risk situations, also consider a professional security sweep.

Frequently asked questions

Why does it say a camera is "possible" instead of "found"?

No phone-based app can be 100% certain. We deliberately avoid false "camera detected" claims and show honest, possible results so you can investigate further.

Why does it need camera or location permission?

The camera is used only for the live infrared/lens preview (processed on-device, never saved). Location is required by Android itself to scan Wi-Fi on older versions — we don't track you. See our Privacy Policy.

I got a lot of magnetic warnings — is that a camera?

Not necessarily. Metal furniture, speakers, and appliances also raise the magnetic field. Use it together with the other detectors.
