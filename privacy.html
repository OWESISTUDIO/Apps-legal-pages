TeacherVault

Privacy Policy

How we handle your information, what stays on your device, and what we share with the services that keep TeacherVault working.

Last updated: 23 May 2026

TeacherVault (“we”, “us”, “the app”) is built by Owesi Studio for Pakistani government school teachers. This policy explains what information the app handles, why, and what control you have over it. It applies to the TeacherVault Android app (package: com.owesistudio.teachervault).

We treat your records as if they were our own: nothing about your documents, students, lesson plans, or diary entries ever leaves your device. The only information that does leave your device is the technical data described in Section 2, and it goes only to Google services that help us keep the app stable and free.

If you do not agree with this policy, please uninstall the app.

1

What we store on your device (and only there)

The following data is created or stored entirely on your phone or tablet. It is encrypted with AES-GCM-256, the key is held in the Android Keystore (hardware-backed on most devices), and it is never transmitted, backed up to the cloud, or shared with anyone — including us:

  • Your profile: name, CNIC number, school name, designation, joining date, profile photograph.
  • Uploaded documents and scans (degrees, certificates, service records, ID cards, etc.).
  • Lesson plans you create or generate.
  • Diary entries (periods, topics, attendance counts).
  • Your PIN (stored in a derived form that cannot be reversed) and biometric preference.
  • App preferences (theme, language).

If you uninstall the app or use “Reset App” in Settings, all of this is permanently and irrecoverably deleted.

2

What we send to Google (and why)

The app uses several Google services to stay stable, get better, and remain free. These services receive the data described below. We have signed Google’s Data Processing Agreement; Google processes this data on our behalf.

  • Firebase Crashlytics — when the app crashes, we receive an anonymized crash report including the device model, Android version, app version, and a stack trace. No personal documents, profile content, or files are ever included.
  • Firebase Analytics — anonymous app usage metrics (screen views, session length, feature engagement). No CNIC, name, document text, or personally identifiable content is sent.
  • Firebase Performance Monitoring — automatic timing of app startup and screen rendering. No content data.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) — a randomly generated push token (not tied to your identity) so we can send important app notifications. You can disable notifications in your phone’s settings.
  • Firebase Remote Config — the app fetches small configuration values (feature flags, kill switches). No personal data is sent in the request.
  • Google AdMob — to display interstitial ads after certain save actions, the app sends ad requests that include your device’s Advertising ID. You can reset or limit this ID in Android Settings → Privacy → Ads.
  • Google Gemini (only when you use the AI lesson plan generator) — the lesson plan prompt you type (class, subject, topic, learning objectives) is sent to Google’s Gemini AI service so it can generate the plan. We never send your documents, profile, students’ data, or diary content.
3

Third-party services in detail

The services listed above are operated by Google LLC. Their privacy policies apply in addition to this one:

  • Firebase: firebase.google.com/support/privacy
  • AdMob: policies.google.com/technologies/ads
  • Gemini API: ai.google.dev/gemini-api/terms
4

App permissions

The app asks for the minimum permissions needed for its features. Each permission is requested only when the corresponding feature is used:

  • Camera — to take a profile photograph or scan a document.
  • Biometric / Fingerprint — to unlock the app when you have enabled biometric lock.
  • Notifications — to deliver push notifications you have opted into.

We never request location, contacts, SMS, microphone, or call logs.

5

Your rights and choices

You always control your data:

  • Reset App (Settings → App Lock / Reset) immediately and permanently wipes the encryption key, the local database, all encrypted files, and all secure-storage entries. Recovery is impossible by design.
  • Uninstall the app — Android removes all on-device data.
  • Reset or limit your Advertising ID — Android Settings → Privacy → Ads.
  • Opt out of analytics — Crashlytics and Analytics collection respect the Android-level “Limit usage reporting” toggles where supported.
  • Request information about data Google holds about you — contact Google directly via the links in Section 3.

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or California, you have additional rights under GDPR / CCPA respectively (access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction). Because we hold no personal data on our servers, “Reset App” or uninstall is the most direct exercise of these rights.

6

Children’s privacy

TeacherVault is designed for adult school teachers and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any data from children. If you believe a child has used the app, please contact us and we will assist.

7

Security

We take security seriously:

  • All on-device data is encrypted with AES-GCM-256 at rest (database via SQLCipher, files via per-file envelope encryption).
  • The master encryption key is held in the Android Keystore (hardware-backed on devices that support it).
  • Sensitive screens (document viewer, profile, lock screen) use FLAG_SECURE to prevent screenshots and screen recording.
  • The app auto-locks after 5 minutes of inactivity, wiping the master key from memory.
  • Network traffic to Google services uses TLS 1.2 or higher.

No security measure is perfect. If you discover a security issue, please email info@owesistudio.com.

8

Data retention

On-device data is kept until you delete it (per-item delete, Reset App, or uninstall). Data sent to Google services is retained per their respective retention policies; for analytics and crash data this is typically 2–14 months.

9

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be announced in the app via the Help screen and reflected in the “Last updated” date at the top of this policy. Continued use of the app after a change indicates acceptance.
