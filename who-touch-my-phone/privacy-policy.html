who-see-my-phone Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

Overview

who-see-my-phone helps you detect unauthorized access attempts on your own device. This app can capture photos, store incident records, show notifications, and optionally upload incidents to your own Google Drive account if you turn that feature on.

Information the app processes

Depending on the features you enable, Sentinel may process:

  • intruder photos captured by the device camera
  • incident records such as event type, time, and session history
  • optional app-open history collected during an incident session
  • app settings such as camera facing, photo quality, and monitoring toggles
  • optional cloud sync settings
  • optional lock settings such as a local in-app PIN and biometric preference

How information is used

who-see-my-phone uses this information only to provide the app's core security features, including detecting unlock attempts, capturing intruder photos, storing incident history, protecting access to the app, and syncing incidents to your own Google Drive when you request that feature.

Camera and photo handling

The camera is used only for the security features you enable inside the app. Captured photos are stored on your device in the app's internal storage. The app does not upload photos to the developer or any third-party server. If you turn on Google Drive sync, incident data and photos are uploaded only to your own Google Drive account.

Device admin, lock events, and usage access

If you grant Device Admin permission, Sentinel can receive failed device unlock events reported by Android. If you enable certain monitoring options, the app may also monitor screen or unlock events and may collect limited app-usage context during an incident session to show you which apps were opened after an event. These permissions are used only for the app's security functionality on your own device.

Cloud sync

Google Drive sync is optional. If enabled, incidents are uploaded to a folder in your own Google Drive account. Sentinel does not operate a remote server for storing your incidents. Turning off sync stops future uploads. Content already stored in your Google Drive remains subject to your Google account settings until you delete it.

Sharing and disclosure

who-see-my-phone does not sell your personal data. The app does not share intruder photos or incident records with the developer. Data may be shared only when you explicitly choose to share a photo, save a photo to your gallery, send a support email, or enable syncing to your own Google Drive account.

Storage and retention

Incident records remain on your device until you delete them, clear app data, or uninstall the app. You can also remove incident items from inside the app. If cloud sync is enabled, copies stored in your Google Drive remain there until you delete them from Drive.

Security

The app is designed to keep incident photos in app-controlled storage and uses local protections for stored content. No method of storage or transmission is guaranteed to be completely secure, but we aim to limit access to data to the minimum needed for app functionality.

Children

who-see-my-phone is not directed to children and is intended for device owners who want to monitor access to their own devices.

Your choices

You can disable monitoring toggles, remove Device Admin access, turn off cloud sync, delete incidents, clear app data, or uninstall the app at any time. You can also choose whether to enable biometric unlock and whether to keep cloud sync active.

Contact

For privacy questions or requests, contact: support@sentinelwatcher.com

Policy updates

This policy may be updated when app features or legal requirements change. The latest version published for the app should be the version used for Google Play listing and in-app disclosure.
